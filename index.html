Airbnb listing counts difference in Seattle, WA, by neighborhood

Between October 2020 and September 2021

By Sharmila Kuthunur and Yuriko Schumacher


The following map shows the difference in Airbnb listings in Seattle neighborhoods between a 12 month timeframe. The map reflects a steep decline in Downtown area, which lost more than 200 listings, and an increase in listings in University District.

Hover over the areas to see the difference in numbe of listings for each neighborhood.


Downtown area is famously known as the home of Amazon's campus, as well as the Space Needle, a well-known tourist location. The loss of Downtown's Airbnb listings could be attributed to Amazon's continuing work from home policy, prompting employees to move to other states with low cost of living.

According to The Seattle Times, tourism in Seattle dropped in urban areas like Downtown and increased, surprisingly, in rural areas around lakes and parks. In 2020, Seattle had the second lowest hotel occupancy rate at 30.8%, with the last place being Vancouver, B.C.

The rise in Airbnb listings near the university district, where the University of Washington campus is located, could be attributed to the return of in-person classes, prompting students and their families to occupy homes and hotels in the area.
